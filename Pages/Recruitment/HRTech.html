Is AI The Future Of Work?

The idea of smart machines and artificial intelligence has been around for a long time, with known stories of smart machines and artificial beings dating back to ancient Greece. AI as a proper scientific field of study was established way back in 1955 and has had its ups and downs since then. Although presently AI is not quite what Isaac Asimov and Philip K. Dick envisioned, you could certainly make the argument that it's on its way to that point.

But what does that mean for the future of work and workers?

What is AI?

A rudimentary understanding of what AI is is necessary to grasp its significance and why it is taking the world by storm. The real-world definition of what AI is substantially different from what the great twentieth-century science fiction writers envisioned in their works. In our reality, AI is not about machines with consciousness and self-awareness, as much as it is about their ability to perform simpler functions.

Britannica defines AI as "the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks commonly associated with intelligent beings." The explanation of why AI technology would be extremely appealing is contained in that definition - it provides people with the opportunity to avail themselves of more powerful and complex tools for doing essential tasks. Private individuals, businesses, and governments have taken a huge interest in it, and as a result, it has had a boom in the last few decades.

How is AI Affecting Jobs?

It wouldn't be too much of a stretch to say that by now, AI has become an integral part of our daily lives, workplaces, and everything we do. This has prompted the MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab to research how it affects the organization of tasks within occupations by analyzing 170 million jobs in the 2010-2017 period. The findings of the resulting study have been fascinating, to say the least.

As a result of advancements in AI and the automation opportunities it offers, wages for workers in manufacturing and production have fallen by more than five thousand dollars during the studied period. This represents the market's reaction to AI as a tool and the value it brings to businesses compared to workers performing the same menial tasks. When it comes to manufacturing and production, there is a lot of work that consists largely of simple tasks that could be automated and performed by machines run by AI.

At our current technological level, AI-driven work can be much more cost-effective than that of humans, making it commercially preferable in many situations. In a sense, employers are taking parts of the regular worker's job and outsourcing it to the machines that can do it more efficiently and reliably. The process bears a striking resemblance to what happened during the industrial revolution, and the end result is the same - high-quality products can be produced at a reduced cost of human involvement.

Conversely, MIT-IBM Watson AI Lab found that the widespread use of AI doesn't affect all jobs in a similar manner. Wages for jobs involving industry-specific knowledge, for instance, have increased by more than six thousand dollars on average for the studied period. The pay of high-wage business employees, narrowly specialized and professionals, and financiers seem to have been boosted by AI rather than deflated.

Most other jobs fall somewhere between those two points. Reassuringly, the study indicates that very few jobs are actually being phased out entirely due to the use of AI as of now. The research pointed out that only one in forty of jobs consist entirely of tasks suitable for AI to perform. These mainly consist of work that is focused on verifying credentials and allowing only certain people to enter a restricted area - such as a ticket taker.

What are the implications of AI for Our Work?

With this in mind, the central question should no longer be whether AI is the future of work or not, because as it stands, AI is very much an essential part of our day to day lives already. The more pertinent question seems to be what does that mean for us as people, as employees and as employers.

Unlike the previous industrial revolution, the one that is currently going on doesn't seem to be as threatening to people's jobs. For a start, it doesn't seem to threaten to make entire sections of the workforce obsolete - it just provides employers with more tools to boost effectiveness.

AI is always being improved and is currently moving forward at breakneck speed. However, it does have its limitations. As it stands, it is very good at performing a limited number of very specific tasks in its programming but is incapable of intuition, ingenuity, inspiration, and innovation. All of these things are often essential for dealing with situations involving unforeseen complications, and a staggering percentage of cases in everyday life are just such occasions.

This means that human employees are still essential for even the most advanced businesses - and would doubtlessly continue to be for the foreseeable future. However, that doesn't mean that their positions and functions will remain the same. Most people would not be so much forced out of their jobs by AI, as they would be forced to learn to use their newly available AI tools to perform their tasks more efficiently.

Technology boosts efficiency and allows workers to perform some tasks at a heretofore unimagined pace, and as a result, the value of the tasks which AI can't perform increases. This is why professions that are grounded in intuition and insight are thriving in this new AI-driven age.

So, employees will have to adapt to use the new high-tech tools to maintain their jobs. But they're not the only ones who are going to have to rethink their strategy and general way of approaching problems and making the most of all available tools. Providing opportunities for life-long learning for employees has already become a necessity for employers, who are also going to have to keep pace with the times.

As new and more advanced AI tools emerge, they're going to have to adopt said tools in order to stay competitive. Due to the pace at which AI technology advances, it is no longer viable just to bring new people into the company every time a new and essential piece of tech is rolled out. This means that employers are going to want to teach their loyal employees to use said AI reliably.

Investments in talent have been a common corporate practice for a long time now and are now viewed as an essential part of the long-term development of a company.

What are the Best Business Applications for AI?

At its current level, AI is very good at performing relatively simple tasks over and over. It doesn’t tire, it doesn’t get bored, it doesn’t stop for any reason, and it can perform said simple tasks at a pace unimaginable to any human. Additionally, some simple tasks performed by AI can be chained one after the other to achieve some rather complex results.

An employer can use this to ensure that their employee isn't buried in simple routine work and is free to do what humans do better than machines. And what flesh and blood employees do better than AI use their judgment and broader scope to react to extraordinary circumstances that simply haven't been factored into the machine's code.

Nearly every industry can benefit from implementing the latest AI solutions in some way or another. It doesn't matter what sort of product you provide - there is something that AI can do for you. For instance, an AI-driven voice-activated maintenance app has made the lives of Japanese wind turbine inspectors a lot easier.

And even when you’re not dealing with outdoor activities and physical labor, AI is a boon. From the perspective of human resources management, AI-driven tools have made it possible for vast amounts of applicant and employee data to be compiled, sorted, and analyzed at a fraction of the effort that doing so manually would cost. This leaves HR specialists with the task of taking said data into consideration, analyzing it, and making the best possible informed decisions based on it when it comes to both hiring and managing human resources. All things considered, AI gives HR employees a break - which they desperately need to deal with the complications that COVID-19 brings into the current situation.

How to Implement AI Solutions?

The ways in which a business can benefit from implementing AI-based solutions depends on the type of activities said business performs. Businesses that are based largely on the routine and efficient performance of sets of simple tasks in perpetuity can reap the greatest rewards when it comes to automation. Production lines can be crewed by AI-operated robots and just overseen and maintained by human specialists. However, AI is useful for much more than that.

Data pertaining to human resources, marketing, and a variety of other aspects of business can be gathered and analyzed by algorithms before it ever reaches the eyes of the people who need it to make critical decisions. The possibilities for the use of AI in modern business are practically endless. Figuring them out requires some hard thinking and more than a bit of ingenuity and creativity

One thing to keep in mind when dealing with AI is that, like any other type of technology, AI needs to be introduced to any environment gradually. Getting the hang of a large variety of quirky, complex, and specialized tools is a demanding endeavour for employees. This is why businesses currently have the practice to routinely add more AI-driven solutions to their portfolio and gradually train their employees in the use of the newest tools.

In Conclusion

Everyone, from the newest startup to the largest billion-dollar corporation, stands to gain from implementing more sophisticated AI-driven tools. However, it should be noted that at the end of the day, AI is just that - a tool to be used by humans. If programmed correctly, it is an excellent tool - and is always getting better. However, it has very real limitations in its application.

Like any new type of technology, there are some very real moral, ethical, and practical problems that AI, its implementation, and its results pose. Discussions and research on the subject are sure to be a fascinating subject in the decades to come. For now, one thing is clear - AI can be used to achieve feats unthinkable to regular humans, but it is still a long way from replacing them at work.
